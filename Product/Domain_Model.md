# Purpose

This document defines the core business domains and entities that make up SafeHaven.

The Domain Model represents the business language of the application.

It intentionally describes business concepts rather than database tables or implementation details.

Engineering, Product, Design, and AI should all use this document as the common vocabulary when discussing SafeHaven.

---

# Domain Philosophy

SafeHaven models the real world.

Everything inside the application should represent something that exists in the homeowner's life.

Examples include:

- A household
- A room
- A refrigerator
- A receipt
- A warranty
- A maintenance task

The goal is to create a complete digital representation of a home and everything inside it.

# Core Domains

## Household

The Household is the root domain of SafeHaven.

Everything belongs to a household.

### Responsibilities

- Household information
- Membership
- Permissions
- Shared ownership
- Settings

---

## Member

Represents a person who belongs to a household.

A member may have different permissions.

Examples

- Owner
- Administrator
- Adult Member
- Child
- Guest

---

## Room

Represents a physical area within a household.

Examples

- Kitchen
- Living Room
- Garage
- Basement
- Shed
- Office

Rooms provide organization and search capabilities.

---

## Inventory

Represents every owned asset.

Examples

- Refrigerator
- Lawn Mower
- Television
- Sofa
- Smoke Detector
- Air Compressor

Inventory is one of the primary domains within SafeHaven.

---

## Category

Categories organize inventory.

Examples

- Appliances
- Electronics
- Furniture
- HVAC
- Plumbing
- Outdoor Equipment

Categories simplify browsing and search.

---

## Document

Represents information associated with an inventory item.

Document Types

- Receipt
- Manual
- Warranty
- Invoice
- Insurance
- Certificate

Documents should always be associated with an inventory item whenever practical.

---

## Photo

Represents images associated with an inventory item.

Examples

- Product photos
- Serial numbers
- Installation photos
- Damage photos

Photos improve identification and documentation.

---

## Warranty

Represents warranty information.

Includes

- Coverage period
- Provider
- Expiration
- Terms
- Claim information

---

## Maintenance

Represents recurring or one-time maintenance.

Examples

- Replace furnace filter
- Change smoke detector batteries
- Winterize sprinkler system
- Clean dryer vent

---

## Maintenance Event

Represents completed maintenance.

Examples

- Completed date
- Notes
- Cost
- Performed by
- Photos

Maintenance Events become part of the permanent ownership history.

---

## Service History

Represents professional work performed on an item.

Examples

- Repairs
- Installations
- Inspections
- Warranty work

---

## Purchase

Represents acquisition information.

Includes

- Purchase date
- Retailer
- Cost
- Payment method
- Order number

---

## AI Suggestion

Represents recommendations generated by AI.

Examples

- Suggested category
- Suggested warranty
- Suggested manufacturer
- Suggested maintenance

AI Suggestions never directly modify data.

User approval is required.

---

## Notification

Represents information requiring user attention.

Examples

- Warranty expiring
- Maintenance due
- AI suggestion available
- Document needs review

---

## Automation

Represents background system behavior.

Examples

- Scheduled reminders
- Recurring maintenance
- AI document analysis
- Future smart home events

Automations assist users without reducing transparency.

# Domain Rules

## Rule 001

Everything belongs to exactly one Household.

---

## Rule 002

Inventory Items are the center of the ownership model.

Most information should connect to an Inventory Item.

---

## Rule 003

Documents should not exist independently without a business purpose.

Whenever practical, every document should be linked to an Inventory Item.

---

## Rule 004

AI generates recommendations.

Users approve changes.

---

## Rule 005

Maintenance belongs to Inventory Items.

Completed maintenance becomes permanent history.

---

## Rule 006

The domain should reflect the homeowner's mental model rather than the database structure.

---

# Future Domains

The domain model is expected to grow.

Potential future domains include:

- Vehicles
- Rental Properties
- Vacation Homes
- Insurance Policies
- Utility Accounts
- Contractors
- Smart Home Devices
- Estate Planning
- Financial Assets

New domains should integrate with the existing model rather than replace it.

---

# Domain North Star

Every business entity should answer one question:

> **Does this represent something meaningful in a homeowner's life?**

If the answer is yes, it likely belongs in the domain model.

If not, it is probably an implementation detail that belongs elsewhere.
