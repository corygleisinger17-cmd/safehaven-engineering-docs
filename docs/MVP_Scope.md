# SafeHaven MVP Scope

> **Version:** 1.0  
> **Status:** Product Commitment  
> **Audience:** Product, Engineering, Design, QA  
> **Last Updated:** July 2026

---

# Purpose

The purpose of the SafeHaven MVP is **not** to build every feature described in the long-term vision.

Its purpose is to prove one simple idea:

> Homeowners will pay for software that helps them organize, protect, maintain, and understand everything they own.

The MVP establishes the trusted ownership foundation that every future capability will build upon.

---

# MVP Goal

When a homeowner finishes onboarding, they should be able to say:

> **"Everything important about my home is finally in one place."**

That is the success criteria.

---

# The Core Experience

Every feature in the MVP should strengthen one simple workflow.

```
Add an Asset
        ↓
Organize It
        ↓
Understand It
        ↓
Protect It
        ↓
Maintain It
        ↓
Find It
        ↓
Receive Helpful Guidance
```

If a feature does not improve this workflow, it probably does not belong in the MVP.

---

# What the MVP Includes

## 1. User Accounts

Users can:

- Register
- Sign in
- Reset passwords
- Manage profiles

The platform should provide secure authentication and account management.

---

## 2. Household Management

Users can:

- Create households
- Invite family members
- Manage household roles
- Share ownership information securely

The household is the primary organizational boundary within SafeHaven.

---

## 3. Property Management

Users can create one or more properties.

Examples include:

- Primary residence
- Vacation home
- Rental property
- Workshop
- Farm
- Cabin

Each property can contain multiple locations.

---

## 4. Room & Location Management

Users can organize assets by location.

Examples:

- Kitchen
- Basement
- Garage
- Shed
- Office
- Closet

Future features depend on knowing where assets are located.

---

## 5. Asset Management

Users can:

- Add assets
- Edit assets
- Archive assets
- Restore archived assets

Assets should support:

- Photos
- Manufacturer
- Model number
- Serial number
- Purchase date
- Purchase price
- Notes

The asset is the foundation of the platform.

---

## 6. Intelligent Classification

SafeHaven should automatically generate:

- Manufacturer suggestions
- Item Type suggestions
- Category suggestions

Users should be able to:

- Review
- Accept
- Reject
- Correct

Accepted classifications become trusted ownership information.

---

## 7. Document Management

Assets should support attached documents.

Supported document types include:

- Receipts
- Manuals
- Warranty documents
- Invoices
- Photos

Document uploads should support future AI extraction.

---

## 8. Warranty Tracking

Users should be able to:

- Store warranty information
- Track expiration dates
- View warranty status
- Receive expiration reminders

Missing warranty documentation should be identified whenever possible.

---

## 9. Maintenance Management

Users should be able to:

- Create maintenance schedules
- Complete maintenance
- View maintenance history
- Track maintenance costs
- Receive reminders

Maintenance history should remain permanently attached to the asset.

---

## 10. Search

Users should be able to quickly find:

- Assets
- Documents
- Manufacturers
- Item Types
- Rooms
- Warranties
- Maintenance history

Search should be fast, forgiving, and useful.

---

## 11. Dashboard

The dashboard should immediately communicate what needs attention.

Examples include:

- Upcoming maintenance
- Expiring warranties
- Missing documentation
- Recently added assets
- Household activity
- Open reminders

The dashboard should answer:

> "What should I do today?"

---

## 12. Notifications

SafeHaven should notify users about:

- Maintenance due
- Warranty expiration
- Household invitations
- Classification reviews
- Important ownership events

Notifications should help—not annoy.

---

## 13. Subscription & Billing

The MVP should include:

- Subscription plans
- Free trial
- Billing management
- Usage limits
- Account cancellation

A business model is part of the MVP.

---

## 14. Account & Data Management

Users should have complete control over their information.

The MVP should support:

- Account deletion
- Data export
- Privacy controls

Ownership includes ownership of personal data.

# MVP Success Metrics

The MVP should be measured by homeowner outcomes, not feature count.

Success includes:

- Time to first asset
- Assets added per household
- Documents attached per asset
- Classification acceptance rate
- Maintenance completion rate
- Warranty tracking adoption
- Weekly active households
- Household invitations accepted
- Subscription conversion
- User retention

Most importantly:

> Do homeowners feel more organized and more confident than they did before using SafeHaven?

If the answer is yes, the MVP has succeeded.

---

# Definition of Done

The MVP is complete when a homeowner can:

- Create a household
- Organize their home
- Add meaningful assets
- Attach documents
- Accept intelligent classifications
- Track warranties
- Track maintenance
- Find information instantly
- Receive helpful reminders
- Share ownership with family members

At that point, SafeHaven has achieved its first promise.

The platform can then begin evolving into the ownership intelligence system described in the long-term vision.

---

# Final Statement

The MVP is not the destination.

It is the first trustworthy step toward building the world's most intelligent home ownership platform.
